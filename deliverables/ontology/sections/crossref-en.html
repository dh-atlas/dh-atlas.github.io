Best-manuscript editionni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/BestManuscriptEdition

An edition based on the supposedly best manuscript, typically the earliest and/or best preserved manuscript in the tradition (Parvum lexicon stemmatologicum).

Chapterni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/Chapter

Medieval and early modern deed, including cartularies (Catalog of Digital Scholarly Editions, Patrick Sahle et al.).

Collected worksni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/CollectedWorks

All works by a specific author (Catalog of Digital Scholarly Editions, Patrick Sahle et al.).

Collection of textsni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/CollectionOfTexts

Different texts by different authors (Catalog of Digital Scholarly Editions, Patrick Sahle et al.).

Critical editionni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/CriticalEdition

An edition in which the text has been constituted on the basis of more than one source according to the genealogical principle. It is based on a strict recension of the manuscript sources and the attempt to edit the archetypal text, and it is thus closely associated with the Lachmannian tradition of textual editing (Parvum lexicon stemmatologicum).

Diaryni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/Diary

A a personal, chronological record of thoughts (Catalog of Digital Scholarly Editions, Patrick Sahle et al.).

Digital editionni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/DigitalEdition

A dynamic edition that fully leverages the capabilities of the digital medium. It should not be confused with a digitized edition, which refers the conversion of a printed edition into an electronic format (Parvum lexicon stemmatologicum).

Diplomatic editionni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/DiplomaticEdition

An edition which is based on a single manuscript and usually follows it very closely in matters of orthography, so that it is suited for e.g. linguistic studies. In many cases, obvious mistakes are not corrected, but perhaps only pointed out in the apparatus (Parvum lexicon stemmatologicum).

Documentary editionni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/DocumentaryEdition

An edition based on a single manuscript, often the supposedly best manuscript, but in some cases also a manuscript of particular literary or linguistic value. It may regularize the orthography of the source (Parvum lexicon stemmatologicum).

Eclectic editionni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/EclecticEdition

An edition which is based on more than one manuscript and tries to approximate an earlier stage of the textual transmission by selecting readings from several manuscripts (Parvum lexicon stemmatologicum).

Inscriptionni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/Inscription

Epigraphic document (Catalog of Digital Scholarly Editions, Patrick Sahle et al.).

Letterni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/Letter

A correspondence document (Catalog of Digital Scholarly Editions, Patrick Sahle et al.).

Monotypic editionni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/MonotypicEdition

An edition based on a single manuscript (Parvum lexicon stemmatologicum).

Paperni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/Paper

Serial documentsni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

Single manuscriptni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/SingleManuscript

Author manuscript(s) under a single work. Includes multi-volume series (Catalog of Digital Scholarly Editions, Patrick Sahle et al.).

Single workni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/SingleWork

A specific work with all its expressions, editions, manuscripts (Catalog of Digital Scholarly Editions, Patrick Sahle et al.).

Synoptic editionni back to ToC or Named Individual ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/dh-atlas/SynopticEdition

An edition which renders the text of two or more manuscripts in parallel columns across one or two pages, or above each other, in horizontal rows, making textual comparison easy for the reader (Parvum lexicon stemmatologicum).
